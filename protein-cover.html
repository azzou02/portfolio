Protein Cover Art

I chose to create a cover art theme reminiscent of "Where's Waldo?" because it offers a playful yet effective way to engage viewers while conveying complex scientific ideas. This theme allowed me to creatively depict the intricacies of protein folding within cellular systems through a factory, making the scientific content more relatable and visually captivating.The vibrant orange hues symbolize the adaptability and resilience seen in sHsp (small heat shock proteinA) complexes. These proteins play a crucial role in maintaining cellular health by responding to stress and ensuring proper protein folding, and the orange tones capture their dynamic, protective nature. In the design, I illustrated the protein-making process as a factory system, emphasizing the assembly line nature of protein synthesis. Within this system, I highlighted a specific protein in a different color to indicate when a protein has gone awry, illustrating the malfunction or misfolding that can lead to cellular issues. This contrasting element not only draws attention to the anomaly but also underscores the importance of sHsps in identifying and managing such errors.
