For the People

This oil painting deliberately emulates the style of communist propaganda posters, to add more to my exploration of bravery, perception, and societal influence through this painting. The composition centers on me, wearing a worker's cap typical of communist iconography. My posture and expression evoke the idealized, confident worker often depicted in such posters. Two birds of prey frame the scene, possibly representing freedom or authority, depending on one's viewpoint. Moreover, by adopting the visual language of communist posters - bold colors, heroic posturing, and symbolic imagery - the painting invites viewers to consider how propaganda shapes our understanding of reality. I used this stylistic choice to reinforce how education (or lack thereof) and cultural context influence our interpretation of symbols and situations. Overall, the artwork highlights the malleability of perception and the subjective experience of truth. I want to challenge viewers to examine their biases and the lenses through which they view the world, politics, and concepts of heroism or danger.
