Mass

2025

Influenced by Vilém Flusser’s theories on technical images, this piece simulates a crowd united only by their disconnection from physical reality. It visualizes the homogenization of mass society through the lens of screen addiction. As we scroll through an endless stream of content, our threshold for engagement rises; it now requires the grotesque or the extreme to break our trance.

The work displays a procession of digital figures, each isolated in their own luminous loop. Amidst this drift, a single figure periodically turns off their phone. This pause is not just a disconnection, but an act of defiance against the economy of attention. It questions whether we are still walking our own paths, or merely scrolling through a pre-determined existence.
